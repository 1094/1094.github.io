. . .

ants live such simple lives,
gather food, eat, work, do they sleep?
do they feel the pain when their brother is stepped on?
or fried with a piece of glass?
do they shed a tear for the millions lost at war?

in and out of their hill
what are they working on?
they scrabble past one and another
but do they stop and speak?
share the joys and pains of another year past?
how many make it to a year?

do they march in rows and dig holes for those past?
or water the battleground where the bodies lay?

would they laugh at us humans, morning for more?
one more day, one more goodbye?
one last hug or smile?

are they stronger for their ignorance?
happier in their bliss?

bred by the millions is one really missed?
can they really tell the difference when bob doesn't show up?
do their hearts bleed, or mothers scream?

some things we were never meant to know
people come, then they go
but being forgotten like ant #12
that would be the only real lost

rest in peace juan
