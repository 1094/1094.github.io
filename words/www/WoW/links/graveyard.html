graveyard shift

my day started early
with a flashlight
cutting through the
darkest that remained

the sun rose slowly
there was no rush
as man-made light
bounded from stone
to stone, from cross
to cross, sinking into
cuts that made names

i searched the names
some deep, other swallow
the faded and the fresh
tip toeing over grass-free
patches, looking, searching
one name, not any name

soon enough my flashlight
wasn't needed, the sun's
rays were bright enough
but i found the name
it belonged to her
she is alive, just her
name is dead to me

i found the stone
moments before the sun
danced high enough to
to light the graveyard
and my body as i lay
still, a top someone's
grave, someone with her name

i laid still under the sky
on top of her grave
quietly watching the never
ending picture show
the clouds put on for me

a box of newports later
countless stares later
a new calmness later
twelve hours later
i arose, stood & sighed
and followed a path
seen by the flashlight's
bouncing yellow as i walked
back to my car, wondering
if i'll have the time
to do it again
